Elementary School

As a child, I was constantly building—whether it was intricate LEGO creations or crafting with construction paper and tape. Even then, I knew I wanted to be an engineer, driven by the joy of turning ideas into reality—a passion that still fuels me today.

Middle School

In middle school, I discovered the magic of special effects after getting my first computer from Circuit City! Experimenting with code in editing applications made me realize the raw power abstracted lines of code could have, sparking a lifelong fascination with computers and technology.

High School

At Troy High School in Fullerton, CA, I had my first exposure to highly technical classes through the Troy Tech program. From math and physics to circuits and computer science, I had the time of my life while learning and playing water polo!

Fullerton College

At Fullerton College, I continued playing water polo at the intercollegiate level while honing my engineering skills and completing prerequisite classes. This time prepared me for the next step in my academic journey and solidified my passion for problem-solving.

UC San Diego

Transferring to UC San Diego to study Mechanical Engineering was a dream come true. I joined clubs like Engineering World Health and Students for the Exploration and Development of Space, where I found a community of like-minded innovators and expanded my horizons in engineering and space exploration.

Halfway through my Mechanical Engineering degree at UC San Diego, I realized my true passion was in computer science. I eagerly took on programming assignments for my group of friends and sought every opportunity to write code, primarily using MATLAB and Python. This drive carried me through to graduation in 2020 with a 4.0 GPA.

After graduating from UC San Diego, I set my sights on honing my computer science skills, eagerly taking on any coding tasks I could find at work. What started as a way to contribute beyond my role quickly became my new career. With dedication and a drive to learn, I transitioned fully into software engineering, embracing a field where creativity meets logic through mass amounts of code. This journey has been the culmination of a lifelong love for building, problem-solving, and turning abstract ideas into tangible solutions—one line of code at a time.
